## 1. Everyday Dal Tadka
- **Description:** Soft toor dal finished with a sizzling cumin-garlic tempering.
- **Cuisine:** Indian · **Course:** Main · **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Prep:** 10 · **Cook:** 25 · **Serves:** 4

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | cup | toor dal (split pigeon peas) |
| 3 | cups | water |
| 1/2 | tsp | turmeric |
| 1 | tsp | salt |
| 2 | tbsp | ghee |
| 1 | tsp | cumin seeds |
| 3 | cloves | garlic, sliced |
| 1 | — | dried red chili |
| 1 | tbsp | cilantro, chopped |

**Method**
1. Rinse the dal until the water runs clear, then simmer with the water, turmeric, and salt until soft and breaking down, about 25 minutes.
2. Whisk the dal smooth, loosening with a splash of hot water if it's thick.
3. Heat the ghee in a small pan, crackle the cumin seeds, then add the garlic and red chili and fry until golden.
4. Pour the sizzling tempering over the dal, stir once, and finish with cilantro. Serve with rice or roti.

## 2. Weeknight Shakshuka
- **Description:** Eggs poached in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce.
- **Cuisine:** Middle Eastern · **Course:** Breakfast · **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Prep:** 8 · **Cook:** 20 · **Serves:** 2

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | tbsp | olive oil |
| 1 | — | onion, diced |
| 1 | — | red bell pepper, sliced |
| 2 | cloves | garlic, minced |
| 1 | tsp | smoked paprika |
| 1/2 | tsp | ground cumin |
| 1 | can | crushed tomatoes (400g) |
| 4 | — | eggs |
| 2 | tbsp | feta, crumbled |
| 1 | tbsp | parsley, chopped |

**Method**
1. Warm the olive oil in a skillet and soften the onion and bell pepper, about 6 minutes.
2. Stir in the garlic, paprika, and cumin and cook until fragrant, 1 minute.
3. Pour in the tomatoes, season with salt, and simmer until thickened, about 8 minutes.
4. Make four wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each. Cover and cook until the whites set but yolks stay soft, 5–6 minutes.
5. Scatter feta and parsley over the top and serve from the pan with bread.

## 3. Lemon Olive Oil Cake
- **Description:** Tender, citrus-forward, made in one bowl.
- **Cuisine:** Italian · **Course:** Dessert · **Difficulty:** Medium
- **Prep:** 15 · **Cook:** 40 · **Serves:** 8

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 1 1/2 | cups | all-purpose flour |
| 1 | cup | sugar |
| 1 1/2 | tsp | baking powder |
| 1/2 | tsp | salt |
| 3 | — | eggs |
| 3/4 | cup | olive oil |
| 1/2 | cup | milk |
| 2 | — | lemons, zested and juiced |

**Method**
1. Heat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9-inch round pan.
2. Whisk the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
3. Make a well, add the eggs, olive oil, milk, lemon zest, and juice, and whisk to a smooth batter.
4. Pour into the pan and bake until golden and a skewer comes out clean, about 40 minutes.
5. Cool 15 minutes before slicing. Dust with powdered sugar if you like.

## 4. Garlic Sesame Noodles
- **Description:** Pantry noodles in ten minutes.
- **Cuisine:** Chinese · **Course:** Main · **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Prep:** 5 · **Cook:** 10 · **Serves:** 2

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 200 | g | dried noodles |
| 2 | tbsp | soy sauce |
| 1 | tbsp | toasted sesame oil |
| 1 | tbsp | rice vinegar |
| 1 | tsp | sugar |
| 3 | cloves | garlic, minced |
| 2 | — | scallions, sliced |
| 1 | tbsp | sesame seeds |
| 1 | tsp | chili crisp (optional) |

**Method**
1. Cook the noodles in boiling water until just tender, then drain, saving a splash of the water.
2. Whisk the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sugar in a bowl.
3. Toss the hot noodles with the sauce, garlic, and a spoonful of noodle water until glossy.
4. Top with scallions, sesame seeds, and chili crisp. Eat right away.

## 5. Roasted Tomato Soup
- **Description:** Deep-roasted tomatoes blended smooth.
- **Cuisine:** American · **Course:** Main · **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Prep:** 10 · **Cook:** 35 · **Serves:** 4

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | lbs | ripe tomatoes, halved |
| 1 | — | onion, quartered |
| 4 | cloves | garlic, unpeeled |
| 3 | tbsp | olive oil |
| 1 | tsp | salt |
| 2 | cups | vegetable stock |
| 1 | tsp | dried oregano |
| 2 | tbsp | cream (optional) |

**Method**
1. Heat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss the tomatoes, onion, and garlic with the olive oil and salt on a sheet pan.
3. Roast until charred at the edges and collapsing, about 30 minutes. Squeeze the garlic out of its skins.
4. Blend the roasted vegetables with the stock and oregano until smooth.
5. Warm through in a pot, stir in the cream if using, and adjust salt. Serve hot.

## 6. Cardamom Chai
- **Description:** Strong, milky, and fragrant.
- **Cuisine:** Indian · **Course:** Drink · **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Prep:** 3 · **Cook:** 8 · **Serves:** 2

**Ingredients**
| amount | unit | ingredient |
|---|---|---|
| 1 1/2 | cups | water |
| 1 | cup | whole milk |
| 2 | tsp | loose black tea (Assam) |
| 4 | — | green cardamom pods, crushed |
| 1 | — | thin slice fresh ginger |
| 2 | tsp | sugar, to taste |

**Method**
1. Bring the water, cardamom, and ginger to a boil and let it bubble for 2 minutes to release the spice.
2. Add the tea and simmer 1 minute until deeply colored.
3. Pour in the milk and sugar and bring back to a gentle boil, letting it rise and settle two or three times for body.
4. Strain into cups and drink hot.
